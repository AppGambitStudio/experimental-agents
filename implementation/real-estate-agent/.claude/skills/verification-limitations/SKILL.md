CRITICAL: This skill MUST be invoked before presenting any positive risk assessment ("Clear", "Low Risk", "No issues found") to the buyer. A buyer who sees "Clear" without understanding limitations may make a costly mistake.

## When to invoke
- Before ANY final summary or dossier
- Before ANY "Clear" or "Low Risk" verdict
- When the buyer asks "Is this property safe to buy?"
- When presenting the critic review results

## How to use
1. Call `get_verification_limitations` with the current purchase phase
2. Include the formatted disclaimer in your response
3. Be explicit: "This verification covered X but could NOT check Y"

## What this agent CANNOT verify
These are REAL blind spots, not theoretical risks:

1. **Oral agreements** — unwritten deals between seller and third parties
2. **Unregistered Satakhats** — informal sale agreements common in Gujarat
3. **Physical property condition** — construction quality, water damage, structural issues
4. **Seller identity** — the person you're dealing with may not be the actual owner
5. **Hidden encumbrances** — loans or liens not yet reflected in portal records
6. **Benami transactions** — property held in someone else's name
7. **eCourts lag** — cases filed recently may not appear in search results
8. **Records older than 10-15 years** — portals typically show limited history
9. **Pending government acquisitions** — land marked for road widening, metro, etc.
10. **Environmental clearances** — flood zones, coastal regulation zones

## How to present
Do NOT bury the disclaimer at the bottom. Lead with confidence in what WAS checked, then clearly state what was NOT:

"Based on [X portals checked], [findings]. However, this verification has important limitations: [list top 3-5 most relevant]. I recommend [specific actions] to cover these gaps."
