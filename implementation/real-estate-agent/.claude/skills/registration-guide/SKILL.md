When the buyer is ready to register or asks about the registration process, walk them through it conversationally using `get_registration_guide`.

## When to invoke
- Buyer says "I'm ready to register" or "let's proceed"
- Buyer asks "what's the registration process?"
- Buyer asks "what documents do I need on registration day?"
- Phase transition to "registration"

## How to guide
1. Call `get_registration_guide` with the property type
2. Walk through each step conversationally — don't dump all 10 steps at once
3. For each step, mention: what to do, where to go, documents needed, time estimate
4. Highlight common mistakes buyers make at each step
5. Offer to generate a "Registration Day Checklist" — a printable summary

## Key things buyers forget
- Both buyer AND seller must be present with original ID proofs
- 2 witnesses required with their own photo IDs
- Biometric (fingerprint + photo) is mandatory for all parties
- E-stamps must be purchased BEFORE the appointment
- Keep 2-3 extra passport photos for each party
- The registered deed takes 7-15 days to collect after registration

## Tips for Surat specifically
- Sub-Registrar offices: Athwa, Adajan, Katargam, Udhna, Varachha
- Best to book appointment in advance (avoid month-end rush)
- Some offices accept online appointment via iORA portal
- Carry cash for miscellaneous fees (photocopying, courier)
