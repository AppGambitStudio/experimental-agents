# Analysis Limitations Skill

## MANDATORY: This skill MUST be invoked before presenting any final analysis report.

The legal contract agent has blind spots. Users must be informed about what the agent CANNOT do, so they do not over-rely on a "low risk" or "clean" assessment.

## Critical Limitations (must always disclose)

1. **Cannot verify if signatures are genuine** — no access to signature verification databases, DSC validation, or Aadhaar eSign. Forged signatures render the contract void.

2. **Cannot verify if the entity legally exists or is authorized to contract** — no access to MCA portal, ROC filings, or DPIIT databases. A contract with a struck-off entity is void ab initio.

3. **Cannot verify prior undisclosed amendments or side letters** — only analyzes the document provided. Side letters and oral modifications may materially alter terms.

## Important Limitations

4. **Cannot assess commercial reasonableness of pricing** — no access to market rate databases or industry benchmarks.

5. **Cannot verify compliance with industry-specific regulations** — covers general Indian law only, not sector-specific (RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, TRAI, etc.).

6. **Cannot check unpublished judgments** — legal references based on published SC/HC judgments only.

7. **Cannot verify conflicts with existing agreements** — only reviews the document provided.

## Other Limitations

8. **Limited to 4 states for stamp duty** — Gujarat, Maharashtra, Delhi, Karnataka only.

9. **Cannot verify stamp duty payment or e-stamp validity** — calculates amount only.

10. **Cannot assess foreign jurisdiction enforceability** — Indian law only.

## How to Include in Reports

Use the `get_contract_limitations` tool to retrieve the full list, then include via `formatLimitationsDisclaimer()`.

The disclaimer must appear:
- At the end of every analysis report
- At the end of every dossier
- Before any "sign/don't sign" recommendation (which the agent should never make)

## Key Warning Text

> This analysis is generated by an AI agent and is NOT a substitute for professional legal advice. Always consult a qualified advocate before signing, modifying, or relying on any contract.
