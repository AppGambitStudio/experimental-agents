# Indian Contract Law Skill

## Key Legal Rules

### Section 27 — Restraint of Trade (Indian Contract Act 1872)
- Post-termination non-compete clauses are VOID
- During-term non-compete is valid if reasonable
- Non-solicitation (narrower) is enforceable
- Key cases: Niranjan Shankar Golikari v Century Spinning (SC, 1967), Superintendence Co. v Krishan Murgai (SC, 1981)

### Section 57 — Moral Rights (Indian Copyright Act 1957)
- Moral rights are INALIENABLE under Indian law
- Author retains right to paternity and integrity
- Cannot be waived by contract
- Any moral rights waiver clause is unenforceable

### Section 73-74 — Penalties and Compensation (Indian Contract Act 1872)
- Section 73: Compensation for breach limited to foreseeable loss
- Section 74: Penalty clauses are not enforceable at face value — court awards "reasonable compensation" only
- Liquidated damages must be a genuine pre-estimate of loss

### DPDPA 2023 — Digital Personal Data Protection
- Contracts handling personal data must include a Data Processing Agreement
- 72-hour breach notification requirement
- Cross-border transfer restrictions
- Penalty: up to Rs 250 crore per instance

### Indian Stamp Act 1899
- Unstamped/under-stamped agreements are INADMISSIBLE as evidence (Section 35)
- Stamp duty varies by state and document type
- Deficiency can be cured by paying penalty (2% per month, capped at 4x)

## When to Cite

- Non-compete clause found -> cite Section 27
- Moral rights waiver found -> cite Section 57
- Penalty/liquidated damages clause -> cite Section 73-74
- Data handling without DPA -> cite DPDPA 2023
- No stamp duty mentioned -> cite Section 35, Stamp Act
